What is Indoor Air Quality (IAQ)?

Indoor air quality, IAQ is the quality of the air that we breathe inside the buildings such as homes, offices, schools, and other enclosed spaces. According to the Environmental Protection Agency indoor air can be 2 - 5 times more polluted than outside air. This is because indoor air can accumulate various pollutants such as dust, allergens, mold spores, and chemicals from household products, which can lead to poor indoor air quality.

However, air quality can greatly vary depending on the specific indoor and outdoor environments in question. Regular ventilation and cleaning can help improve indoor air quality.

Negative Impacts of Poor Indoor Air Quality

Poor indoor air quality can cause respiratory issues, allergies, asthma, headaches, fatigue, and cognitive impairment. Long-term exposure increases risks of chronic diseases like COPD, heart disease, and lung cancer. Vulnerable groups, including children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing conditions, are particularly affected. It can also spread infections and weaken immunity, emphasizing the need for proper ventilation and pollutant control.

Causes of Poor Indoor Air Quality

Inadequate ventilation: Insufficient fresh air due to poor ventilation system can build up air pollutants in the environment


Indoor pollutants: The most common indoor pollutants include Mold Spores, Pet Dander, Radon, Secondhand Smoke, Lead Particles, Dust Mites, Combustion Pollutants These come from burning materials or fuel for cooking, heating, or fireplace. Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) These are gases that are released from certain solids or liquids, including paints, pesticides, air fresheners, and dry-cleaned clothing, and Formaldehyde: This is a colorless, strong-smelling gas.


Tobacco smoke: People who smoke indoors releases, harmful chemicals, and toxins into the air, and can threaten the life of smokers, or non-smokers


Moisture and mold: Excessive moisture in your home, or office, can lead to the growth of mold and mildew, which can release spores into the air and can lead to respiratory problems and allergies


Building materials: Some of our building materials, including asbestos, lead-based paint, and formaldehyde-containing products, release some toxins in the air and pollute the environment.


HVAC System Issues: Improperly maintained HVAC Systems and dirty coils causes poor air quality.
